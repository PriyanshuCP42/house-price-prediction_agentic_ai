# Real Estate Investment Guide — King County, Washington

## Price-Per-Square-Foot Benchmarking

Price per square foot (PPSF) is the most standardized metric for comparing property values across different sizes and configurations. In King County, PPSF varies dramatically:

- **Budget Tier (< $150/sqft):** Typically older homes in south King County (Federal Way, Auburn, Kent). Often require renovation but represent strong value opportunities. Common in zipcodes 98001-98032.
- **Mid-Market ($150-250/sqft):** The bulk of King County transactions. Includes established neighborhoods in Renton, Kirkland, and outer Seattle. Grade 7-8 homes with standard amenities.
- **Premium ($250-400/sqft):** High-grade homes in desirable areas like Bellevue, Mercer Island, and central Seattle. Grade 9-11 properties with modern finishes and good views.
- **Luxury (> $400/sqft):** Waterfront properties, downtown Seattle condos, Medina estates. Grade 12-13 mansion-level construction. Small market segment with high price volatility.

## Understanding Building Grade

The King County grading system (1-13) is a critical price predictor:
- **Grade 1-4:** Below minimum building standards. Very rare. Significant structural concerns.
- **Grade 5-6:** Below average quality. Basic materials and finishes. Functional but dated.
- **Grade 7 (Average):** Standard residential construction. The most common grade in the dataset. Meets all building codes with standard materials.
- **Grade 8-9 (Good to Better):** Above-average materials, better design. Custom details, hardwood floors, upgraded kitchens.
- **Grade 10-11 (Very Good to Excellent):** High-quality construction. Custom architecture, premium materials throughout. Often includes luxury amenities.
- **Grade 12-13 (Luxury to Mansion):** Exceptional construction quality. Custom-designed homes with the finest materials. Professional landscaping, often with waterfront access.

Each grade increase above 7 typically adds 15-25% to property value, making grade the second strongest price predictor after living area square footage.

## Condition Rating Impact

Property condition (1-5 scale) reflects current maintenance state:
- **Condition 1 (Poor):** Major repairs needed. Deferred maintenance throughout. Typically requires $50K+ in immediate investment.
- **Condition 2 (Fair):** Noticeable wear. Some repairs needed. Functional but needs attention within 1-2 years.
- **Condition 3 (Average):** Normal wear and tear for age. The most common rating. No immediate concerns.
- **Condition 4 (Good):** Well-maintained. Recent updates to some systems. Move-in ready.
- **Condition 5 (Excellent):** Like-new condition. Recent renovation or meticulous maintenance. Premium finishes throughout.

A jump from condition 3 to condition 5 typically adds 8-15% to property value, though the premium varies by neighborhood expectations.

## Investment Red Flags

Watch for these warning signs in property analysis:
- **Old + No Renovation:** Homes over 40 years old with no renovation record likely need significant capital investment (roof, HVAC, plumbing, electrical).
- **Low Grade + High Price:** If a property is priced above neighborhood averages despite a low grade (5-6), investigate why — it may be overpriced or hiding issues.
- **Condition-Grade Mismatch:** A high-grade home in poor condition (grade 10+, condition 1-2) may have suffered neglect of expensive systems.
- **Small Lot + Large House:** Properties where the structure seems oversized for the lot may have expansion or zoning limitations.
- **Price Outlier in Zipcode:** If a property is priced 25%+ above the zipcode median with no clear justification (waterfront, views, etc.), proceed with caution.

## The 1% Rule for Rental Investment

For investors considering rental property: the monthly rent should ideally be at least 1% of the purchase price. Example: a $500,000 home should generate $5,000/month in rent. In King County's high-value market, achieving this ratio is difficult, making many properties better suited for appreciation-based investment strategies rather than cash flow.

## When to Walk Away

Consider avoiding a property investment when:
- Model confidence is below 40% (high prediction uncertainty)
- Risk score exceeds 75/100 (multiple compounding risk factors)
- Price exceeds comparable average by more than 20% without clear justification
- Property requires estimated repairs exceeding 15% of purchase price
- The zipcode shows high price volatility (coefficient of variation > 0.6)
